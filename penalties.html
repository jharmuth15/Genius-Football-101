These are the most common penalties that can happen in an NFL game. Click on one to learn some information
  • Holding
      • Usually invovles the offensive line.
      • Holding is the illegal restraining of another player who is not in possession of the ball.
      • Results in a 10 yard penalty.
      • This is shown in the image here. Number 28 on the Bills is commiting the penalty as he isn't giving the Jets player a chance to get to the Quarterback
  • Clipping
      • Peanlty is usualy against the defense
      • Clipping is the act of a "throwing the body across the back of the leg of an eligible receiver or charging or falling into the back of an opponent below the waist after approaching him from behind, provided the opponent is not a runner." Basically a hit below the belt.
      • Results in a 15 yard penalty
      • This is shown in the image below. The Atlanta Falcons player is hitting the 49ers player very low, at the knees. this play resulted in a 15 yard penalty.

  • Offensive/Defensive Pass Interference
      • Penalty usually against either the reciever or a defensive back trying to defend the ball.
      • Pass interference (PI) is a foul that occurs when a player interferes with an eligible receiver's ability to make a fair attempt to catch a forward pass. Pass interference may include tripping, pushing, pulling, or cutting in front of the receiver, covering the receiver's face, or pulling on the receiver's hands or arms.
      • Either a 10 or 15 yard penalty, based on referee's decision.
      • This is shown in the image below. The Dallas Cowboys player is not allowing the Detroit Lions player to have an oppurtunity catch the ball.
  • Encroachment
      • Penalty is always against the Defense.
      • Encroachment refers to when before the snap, a defensive player illegally crosses the line of scrimmage and makes contact with an opponent or has a clear path to the quarterback
      • Results in a 5 yard penalty against the defense.
      • This is shown in the image below shows an example of encroachment, the Green Bay player went across the line of scrimmage before the ball was snapped
  • Delay of Game
      • Penalty is 95% of the time against the Offense.
      • Delay of Game is when the offensive team is penalized five yards for delay of game if it fails to put the ball in play by either snap or free kick before the play clock expires. It is often 25 seconds from the time the referee signals the ball ready for play. In the National Football League, it can also be 40 seconds from the end of the previous down, depending on the circumstances at the time. Also, the defensive team can be given the same penalty if they fail to quickly yield the ball to officials after a play has ended, prevent a player on the offense from getting up after the play, or kicking the ball.
      • Results in a 5 yard penalty against the offense/defense.
      • This is shown in the image below, the Giants failed to snap the ball before the play clock went to :00.
  • Offsides
      • Penalty is almost always against the defense.
      • Offsides is a foul in which a player is on the wrong side of the line of scrimmage when the ball is snapped. This foul occurs simultaneously with the snap.
      • Results in a 5 yard penalty
      • The image below shows an example of offsides. The Titans player crossed the line of scrimmage before the ball was snapped
  • Facemask
      • Penalty is against both the offense and defense.
      • The face mask is the part of the helmet that directly covers the face. It is a major protection for the players, made of metal covered either with rubber or plastic. A facemask penalthy is tackling or otherwise restraining a player by grabbing the face mask is illegal due to the risk of injury, and the penalty is severe, drawing 15 yards, and also a first down if committed by the defense.
      • Resuls in a hefty 15 yard penalty vs the team that commited the penalty. As players can get hurt by it.
      • The image below shows an example of a facemask penalty. The Saints player is grabbing the helmet of the Panthers player.
  • False Start
      • Penalty is against the offense.
      • A false start penalty is movement by an offensive player (other than the center) after he has taken a set position. For offensive linemen, this movement might be as minimal as a couple of centimeters, although the rule's intent is to prevent offensive players from unfairly drawing the defense offside.
      • Results in a 5 yard penalty

        • There are many other obscure penalties in the NFL, but these are the penalties that are called most often
